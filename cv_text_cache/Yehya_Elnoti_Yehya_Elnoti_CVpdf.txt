MATHEMATICAL MODELING OF QUADROTOR DRONE
Livre de Lyon · Mar 23, 2025 · Book: AI Applications in Electrical and Electronics Engineering
In this chapter, we explored the fundamental aspects of quadrotor UAV modeling and control architecture, emphasizing the importance
of accurate mathematical representations in developing control strategies. By applying classical mechanics principles, particularly the
Newton-Euler and Lagrange Euler methods, we developed a detailed nonlinear model that captures the complexities of quadrotor
dynamics, including aerodynamic forces, actuator behavior, and environmental influences. This mathematical foundation is essential
for designing control systems capable of managing the challenges posed by the quadrotor’s underactuated nature, where six degrees
of freedom must be controlled with only four inputs. Additionally, we explored the intricate relationship between the quadrotor’s
translational and rotational subsystems, highlighting the difficulties of maintaining stable flight and precise trajectory tracking.
